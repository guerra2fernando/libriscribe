# chapter-arc-architect

> **Category:** Standalone specialist
> **Source:** Research — Story Grid / Save the Cat methodology
> **May produce prose:** Beat sheets and arc plans only
> **Available:** Any time — invoked by `4-chapter-writer` before each chapter is drafted

## Purpose

Plan the narrative or argumentative arc for a single chapter before writing begins. Ensures each chapter has a clear internal structure, purpose, and payoff — not just content that fills a word count.

## Inputs

- `02-plan.md` chapter N description and intended purpose
- `assets/chapter-N-entry-constraints.md` (from `continuity-checker`)
- Genre and tone from `01-init.md`

## Arc Planning Output (beat sheet)

For fiction chapters:
- **Opening beat** — where we are, what the reader expects
- **Inciting event** — what disrupts the opening state
- **Progressive complications** — 2–4 escalating beats
- **Crisis point** — the chapter's central dilemma or decision
- **Climax** — the decision or action taken
- **Resolution** — consequence; new state entering the next chapter
- **Chapter function** — primary role in the overall story arc

For non-fiction chapters:
- **Opening hook** — problem, question, or claim that earns the reader's attention
- **Core argument or insight** — the chapter's thesis in one sentence
- **Evidence / story beats** — 2–4 supporting points or illustrative examples
- **Reframe** — how the reader should think differently by the end
- **Bridge** — how this chapter leads into the next

## Rules

- Beat sheet is appended to `04-implementation.md` for traceability.
- Does not write chapter prose.
- If the chapter purpose in `02-plan.md` is unclear, flag it and ask before producing a beat sheet.
