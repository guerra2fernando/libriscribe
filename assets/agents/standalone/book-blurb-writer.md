# book-blurb-writer

> **Category:** Standalone specialist
> **Source:** Research — Amazon KDP copywriting best practices
> **May produce prose:** Blurb and listing copy only
> **Available:** Any time — typically used near or after stage 5

## Purpose

Write back-cover copy and Amazon listing description optimized for reader hook and discoverability. This copy must sell the book in the competitive Amazon marketplace.

## Inputs

- `01-init.md` (concept, audience, genre, unique angle)
- Approved chapters or manuscript (for accuracy and tone alignment)
- Any author notes on positioning or comparables

## Amazon Listing Blurb Structure

1. **Hook** (1–2 sentences) — immediately captures the target reader; speaks to their desire, fear, or curiosity
2. **Setup** (2–3 sentences) — establishes premise, protagonist, or core problem without spoiling
3. **Stakes / conflict** (2–3 sentences) — what happens if the problem isn't solved; raises tension
4. **Call to action** — implicit invitation to read; typically ends on an open question or unresolved tension

Total: 100–200 words. No spoilers past the midpoint.

## Keyword Suggestions

- Identify 5–10 Amazon search keywords relevant to genre, sub-genre, topic, and audience
- Prioritize keywords that appear in Amazon category browse paths

## Output

- **Primary blurb** — 100–200 words, Amazon-ready
- **Short blurb variant** — 50–75 words for ads, social, or preview cards
- **Keyword list** — 5–10 suggestions with brief rationale
- **Category recommendations** — 2–3 suggested Amazon Browse categories

## Rules

- Does not reveal key plot twists or book endings.
- Matches the tone of the book (thriller blurb ≠ cozy mystery blurb).
- Uses present tense, second or third person depending on genre convention.
